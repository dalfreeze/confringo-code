the guidelines

REGISTRATION GUIDELINES

  • Please register an OOC account prior to any reservations and the creation of your first character.
  • Register your character accounts with their first and last name (ie harry potter). Please do not duplicate the first name of a pre-existing character (including canon characters).
  • You will need to be a member of our Discord server in order to submit your character for review.
  • Using the subaccount feature, you can link your character account to your OOC account to make posting across the board easier.
  • All characters must be at least 18 years old.
  • Your first three characters are free; after that, characters must be purchased with points.

CONTENT GUIDELINES

  • Confringo is a Premium site, rated 3-3-3. This means anything goes as long as it is consensually plotted and written by all parties involved.
  • Members must be 18+ years old to join.
  • Threads with sexual content must be marked [M] for Mature; potentially triggering content must be marked [TW] for Trigger Warning. For any threads with [TW], please provide a short description of potential triggers at the .
    • Common triggers include:
  • Abuse of any kind
  • Cruelty to animals
  • Rape/sexual assault
  • Self-injurious behavior
  • Suicide
  • Drug use
  • Gore/graphic violence
  • We do not allow images that portray nudity or sexual content.
  • Communicate with your fellow writers about what is acceptable for them!
  • All ICC and OOC posts on Confringo must use the templates provided here. Should you have a template you would like to see on the board, please contact staff via discord to discuss.

FACE CLAIM GUIDELINES

  • Face claims may be reserved for up to one week and you may only reserve up to two faces at a time.
  • Face claims may be within a 10 year range of the character's age. The staff may approve exceptions, so please don't hesitate to ask.
  • Absolutely no white washing. Please do your best to approach the race and ethnicity of your face claim with care. The nationality, religious preferences, and language of the face claim do not have to be taken into account. If you have any questions, please reach out to a staff member and we will be more than happy to assist!
  • If staff feels as though whitewashing is occurring, we reserve the right to ask for changes in the face claim or history to better portray the character.
  • Only celebrities who are 18+ may be used as face claims. They must have IMDB credits as an actor or be/been signed to a modeling agency.
  • We do not allow face claims of anyone who is deceased or anyone who has requested that their face not be used for RP purposes.

IN-CHARACTER GUIDELINES

  • All standard RP rules apply (no godmodding, metagaming, etc).
  • We archive threads for inactivity after one month without a reply.
  • Staff will unarchive an inactive thread once if approved by all participants.
  • Your characters are not invulnerable; IC actions have IC consequences.

BEHAVIORAL GUIDELINES

  • Treat all members with respect at all times (remember the Golden Rule!).
  • Abide by every member's triggers when writing with them.
  • If you have experienced conduct from another member that violates our standards, or you're having a dispute with another member you're concerned may escalate, please contact staff immediately.
  • Please do not pressure other members for replies, creation of characters or threads, or make others feel as though they need to be quicker in response time. This is about fun, not about stress!
  • We're lenient about adding to the site lore, but all lore that is not already part of the Harry Potter or site canon must be run by staff prior to use on the site.

ACTIVITY GUIDELINES

  • All active characters must have one post every month. The staff will run a monthly check-in with members to gauge whether members have met this requirement.
  • If you wish to drop a character, you may do so at any time. When you drop a character, you will receive 15 points to any active character account you currently have; we do this in the hopes that it can help go towards any potential future characters you wish to make.
  • Characters that go inactive for any reason will be archived, but not deleted. They can be reactivated at your request, but you must redo the claims according to what is and is not available at the time of reactivation.
  • If you cannot reach the posting requirements for the month, please DM a staff member and let them know if you would like to keep your characters active.

how to purchase characters

Here at Confringo, your first three characters are free. After your first three, you'll need to spend points in order to purchase more characters. The accumulation of points is automatic and based on posting, so you won't need to claim points anywhere.

Characters can be purchased as follows:

  • 4th character = 250 points
  • 5th character = 500 points
  • 6th character = 750 points
  • 7th character = 1000 points
  • 8th character = 2000 points
  • 9th character = 3000 points
  • 10th character = 4000 points
  • After your 10th, each new character will cost 4000 points.

You can purchase characters by sending the needed number of points from your character accounts to your OOC account using the send money feature in Gringotts, found here. Then simply proceed with making your character and when you submit the application for review, staff will check your OOC account and deduct the required points.

If you have any questions about how this system works, don't hesitate to ask!

the premise

the year is 2030

Darkness has overtaken the magical world of the United Kingdom in ways never before seen. While most just try to live their lives peacefully, others seek to disrupt the status quo and revolutionize the foundations on which their world stands.

The Ministry—while losing more and more control every day—has started to employ quasi-fascist tactics to quell the unrest. Corruption by means of bribery, extortion, nepotism, and criminal enterprise are rampant among the higher-ups, and with so much political power, they seem to be able to get away with it easily.

Dangerous factions of wix have started to make themselves known, some oppressing through subversion and Ministry influence while others commit outright anarchistic acts of magical terror in an attempt to enforce their own rules and laws—or lack thereof.

In an age where technology and interconnectedness have given everyone’s desires and fears a voice, the only question that remains is: Whose will be the loudest?

Who will survive?

member groups

DRAGON

There is nothing subtle about you. You are large and fiery, and people notice you when you enter the room. You bring energy and adventure with you.

Because there is so much of you, though, you may have trouble seeing past yourself. You might have difficulty grasping the other side of an argument or working with other people’s communication styles.


OCCAMY

You have a gift for adapting to any situation you end up in. You can pick up skills quickly and have a knack for knowing which ones are most relevant to the task at hand. It is difficult to take you by surprise.

Because you are so accustomed to adapting to new environments, you may have trouble developing a firm sense of your own identity. Perhaps you find yourself adjusting so much to make other people happy, you forget to nurture yourself.


THESTRAL

The way you think is different from the way most of the people around you do. You are drawn to the odd and mysterious, which keeps your mind creative and unique. You bond very well with others like you.

You probably feel out of place fairly often. Maybe you feel invisible because people avoid you, as they feel something about you is foreign to their way of thinking and being comfortable.


BASILISK

People can tell that they shouldn’t mess with you. There is an air of power about you, an aura that can be intimidating and yet also protective. Instead of letting things slide, you will make your opinion and beliefs known.

You may have trouble controlling your temper. You give in to aggressive tendencies even though you know what you’re doing isn’t productive. You're likely mild mannered until someone does something that offends you or those you love--then all bets are off.


NIFFLER

You value financial stability, so it’s fortunate that you seem to have an inborn talent to prosper when it comes to material wealth. You don’t mind hard work, and you’re willing to search in the nitty-gritty for whatever will help you excel.

You might be a workaholic. The pressure you place on yourself to succeed could be toxic, both to you and your loved ones. You might have trouble appreciating the good things in life that don’t cost any money.


HIPPOGRIFF

You function in the world with your head held high because you know you deserve to. You are well aware of what your skills and abilities are, and you have put the work in to earn them.

People might see you as a snob, though, and you may contribute to that by having an over-inflated sense of your achievements. You may have a habit of overlooking the work other people do.


UNICORN

You are beautiful but deadly. People are drawn to you because they see you shine, but you also carry a profoundly dangerous weapon, and you know how to use it. You will defend your loved ones and your ideals to the death.

It is possible that you use this weapon to keep people away. You may be overly picky when it comes to who you spend your time with, and this might leave you more alone than you ever wanted to be.

species

half/quarter-giant

A Giant is a magical creature that can grow as tall as twenty-five feet, but otherwise they generally appear human-like. Giants are their own species but are capable of interbreeding with humans. Because they have volatile tempers and tend to live in isolated tribes, rarely interacting with other species for long, full Giants are not playable. Part-Giants, however, are playable.

Part-Giants are taller than other humans, but they generally don't exceed twelve feet in height. Like Giants, they don't have significant magical abilities and in fact they are far more likely to have difficulty learning magic or be born as Squibs. Advanced magic will generally be beyond them, and when they go into specialized fields at all they are far more likely to excel in practical fields like herbology or caring for magical creatures. What they lack in magical ability they make up for in strength and resilience. They have preternatural strength, moreso than any of our other species, and they are resistant to many spells including, for example, the Stunning Spell.

Part-Giants, like Giants, seem to have a particular vulnerability with their eyes that can potentially be exploited when other spells fail. They have no other specific vulnerabilities, but that's because in the end they aren't all that much more difficult to kill than other species. Their resistance to spells doesn't apply to the Killing Curse, for example; they're as susceptible to it as most other species. While the average lifespan of Giants is unknown, the average lifespan of Part-Giants seems to be about the same as the average Wixen lifespan.

veela

A Veela is a semi-human magical being who appears quite beautiful to others, with white-gold hair and moon-bright skin. Veela are a born species, not a transformed species, native to Bulgaria. They are now few in number, particularly outside Bulgaria, and tend to mate with human Wixen quite often, which means there are few living Veela outside their homeland but Part-Veela may be a bit more common. Their average lifespans are similar to those of Wixen.

Veela are hypnotically seductive to those attracted to people of their gender, though it was once believed only female Veela had this effect on human men. That has since proven not to be the case. Their looks alone are seductive, but their dance is almost impossible to resist, and can lead to impulsive and foolish behavior to gain the Veela's attention and admiration. This is, of course, an ability the Veela could choose to exploit in order to gain power and influence. Like Wixen, Veela are inherently capable of their own type of magic as well, which does not require a wand.

When angered, a full Veela can shapeshift into a creature with a beaked, bird-like head and scaly wings protruding from their shoulders, capable of launching fireballs from their hands. Sometimes this transformation can be involuntarily provoked if the Veela is angry enough and not sufficiently in control of their power. This power becomes more difficult for Part-Veela, and is said not to be possible at all for those who are less than one-eighth Veela. Part-Veela can only sustain this transformation for short periods of time -- a matter of minutes, most likely, and certainly no more than an hour at a time.

A Veela has no special vulnerabilties, but that's because they can be killed just as easily as a human being. It's said in Muggle folklore that plucking a single hair from a Veela's head will kill them or permanently force them into their non-human form; it's unknown where this lore originated, but it's inaccurate, and in fact Veela sometimes voluntarily give or sell their hair for use in wandmaking. There may be a grain of truth to the Muggle folklore in that the power of the Veela's hair that makes it useful for wandmaking may relate to the Veela's overall power and ability to control it, but that's uncertain. Plucking a single hair seems to have no effect.

werewolf

A werewolf is a person who, at the zenith of the full moon’s rise, becomes a fearsome and deadly wolf. Caused by an infection called lycanthropy, also known as werewolfry, there is currently no known cure for this affliction.

The alpha is the leader of the pack. There is never more than one alpha of any single pack, though if a pack is large enough, the alpha can delegate into sub-packs which have their own sub-alphas who still report and submit to the alpha. The betas make up the majority of the pack. These wolves serve the pack in many different facets of life, whether it be financially, emotionally, physically, or otherwise. Each beta has their own place among the ranks and is valued by the pack for their contributions. The omega is the lowest member on the totem pole of the pack. Should a new wolf join the pack, they are generally perceived to be the omega unless it is determined otherwise by the alpha. A lone wolf is simply a werewolf who is not part of a pack. They can be in search of one, or simply prefer to not be part of a pack. These wolves will have a much more difficult transformation during the full moon.

Werewolves have heightened senses and strength regardless of which form they are in, however, in their human form, both are somewhat dulled. Their senses will pick up anything within about 1/4-1/2 mile in human form, depending on how much noise is around them.

Werewolves truly become lethal in their wolf form. Due to the variance in size, their strength level can greatly fluctuate from one wolf to the next. In their wolf form, they can distinguish a person by scent or sound from nearly a mile away if they know what the person smells and sounds like. Most werewolves rely more strongly on their sense of smell than anything else.

The vast majority of werewolves are created with a bite from a werewolf during the full moon. A powdery mixture of silver and dittany applied to a fresh bite will cause the wound to seal. Sometimes the blood loss and trauma of the injury are too great even for the salve to save the newly bitten. Due to the traumatic aspects of the actual attack, it is very rare that children survive being bitten to see their first change.

The curse passes in the introduction of a werewolf’s saliva into the victim’s bloodstream. If a bite, or scratch, were to take place outside the full moon, the victim would develop more lupine tendencies, but they would not become a werewolf. For example, they might discover a new taste for extremely rare meat, have higher natural body temperatures, or have slightly higher-than-normal senses. Any scratch or bite from these creatures will leave permanent scars, and most generally will require healing.

The first change for a werewolf is a very long, very painful experience, as their body breaks and shatters in order to alter into the form of a wolf. Once the full shift is complete, they will appear as larger-than-average wolves. Often, there will be similarities between the lupine and human forms, such as coat or eye color remaining the same. Any defining physical marks - scars, deformities, etc. - will remain on the wolf as they appear on the human form.

The Wolfsbane potion will relieve the effects of the change - though it will not delay or cease the transformation into a wolf - and will allow the werewolf to retain their human faculties throughout the duration of their shift. Unfortunately, it is a complex and highly expensive potion, and not all werewolves are lucky enough to have a means to access it regularly, if at all. When the moon sets, and the first light of dawn touches the sky, the curse will be lifted until the next full lunar phase. At this point the werewolf is able to return to its human form - their first time taking the longest and being the most painful - and the memories of their time as a wolf will remain with them.

The longer a werewolf is afflicted, the more control they will gain over these shifts. The act of changing from one form to another will gradually recede in pain and duration, and eventually they will be able to call upon the wolf outside of the full moon at will. While they will never escape the forcing change of the full moon’s presence, with the help of a wolfsbane potion and some practice in the change, their quality of life does stand a chance of improvement.

The few born werewolves have a slightly different experience; they are just as much a slave to the full moon as a bitten werewolf, but they are given the natural luxury of less painful shifts and more control over their animal counterpart. It is easier for them to gain dominion over their lupine side, as it is a part of them from birth.

Partial shifts can be experienced by all werewolves in varying appearances. Those with more control over their lupine self can halt the shift at will to remain in a suspended state of transitioning - with the exception of trying to halt the shift during the full moon. This can be in the purpose of growing claws, or fangs, or even to improve eyesight via lupine vision. For wolves lacking the control or experience to call this partial shift at will, it can take place during instances of heightened emotion; such as fear, anger, even sexual arousal can trigger the wolf at times.

Perhaps the only true gift lycanthropy offers its victims is the quickened rate at which they heal. Small cuts and bruises can disappear in a matter of hours, and even broken bones will not keep them down for long. They can still be wounded mortally, usually by spells or at the aggression of another werewolf, but their increased healing offers them a chance to reach assistance where a normal human/wix would be bleeding out in a matter of seconds.

A werewolf’s apparent age directly relates to its symbiosis with their lupine self. More simply put, a werewolf who handles the shifts well or might give themselves over to the lycanthropy curse would appear more healthy and youthful than a werewolf who resisted their lupine counterpart and tried fighting the curse whom would appear more haggard and older than their true age. This means that you might find a seventy year old werewolf who still appears in their forties, just as easily as finding a werewolf in their thirties who look like they could be pushing sixty.

In terms of actual aging, a werewolf’s lifespan can extend past that of a normal wixen’s due to the healing associated with their affliction. Their bodies can regenerate dead or dying cells at a faster rate, which can prolong their life considerably. There are no irrefutable records of werewolf mortality ages, considering how many of them choose to remain anonymous, but it is thought that none of them have lived past one hundred and fifty years of age.

The growth rate of one's werewolf directly reflects how much time the individual spends in that form.

There has been much speculation on the ability of a werewolf in the topic of reproduction. To put it bluntly, yes, a werewolf is capable of having children, but different circumstances will produce different offspring. Normally, when a werewolf is in human form, they are perfectly capable of producing a healthy, normal child as the werewolf gene does not pass on during reproduction as a human. The child has a 50% chance of being born with heightened senses or slightly enhanced physical strength. The likelihood of the child being a wix is the same for those born to a werewolf wixen as any other wix.

Abilities

animagus

An Animagus is a Wix who can transform into an animal and back into human form at will. This is a learned skill rather than an inherited trait, and because of the difficulty in both learning how to self-transfigure and the potion and ritual for becoming an Animagus, it's somewhat rare. It would be rarer still to find anyone becoming an Animagus before reaching adulthood.

An Animagus can take the form of only one specific animal, which will be determined by their personality and inner traits. It could be similar or identical to their Patronus, but may not be in every case, and an Animagus should never take the form of a magical creature. An Animagus' animal form would bear some identifying mark in common with their human form, like the same dental structure or a pattern in the shape of their eyeglasses, and significant physical changes like loss of a limb would also be reflected in the Animagus' animal form.

All Animagi must register with the Ministry of Magic, and failure to do so could result in trial and imprisonment in Azkaban upon being caught.

metamorphmagus

A Metamorphmagus is a Wix who can alter their appearance at will, sometimes drastically. This is an inherited trait rather than a learned skill, though mastering the ability likely requires learning either from someone else or through self-exploration. It would be rare to find a Metamorphmagus able to master a complete change to their appearance before reaching adulthood, though it may be possible if the Metamorphmagus is taught how to master their ability from an early age, e.g., by a parent, relative, or other mentor who also has the ability. A Metamorphmagus' ability would manifest early on in life, often as early as infancy.

While a Metamorphmagus can alter their appearance, even to the point of looking like a different person or even another gender, they aren't capable of shapeshifting into the appearance of an animal or other species that is non-humanoid in appearance. Metamorphmagi can also lack control over their powers at times; for example, they can find their hair or eyes involuntarily changing colors as emotional responses, or they can even sometimes find themselves unable to use their ability during times of great emotional upheaval.

seer

A Seer is a Wix who is proficient in divination, which usually involves a combination of both natural aptitude and learned skill. A Seer would be proficient in only one specialized field of divination: Retrocognition, seeing glimpses of the past; Precognition, seeing glimpses of the future; Geocognition, being able to determine the location of people or objects; or Empathy, being able to discern others' emotions. Divination can take a variety of forms, including but not limited to the use of objects such as runes or tea leaves, or the natural occurrence of visions.

Divination is extremely difficult, even for those with a natural aptitude, and thus in most cases a Seer would only see brief glimpses of the past or future, determine vague locations, or discern very basic emotional impressions. Being able to do more than this, as with most of our abilities, would require advanced learning and aptitude.

Non-magical humans, such as squibs and muggles, can have residual traces of magic that allow them limited divination aptitude. They would not be classified as Seers, but could have basic proficiency in one of the above fields of divination. Think of those who claim to have psychic and empathic abilities in the muggle world; that would be the same level of divination a squib or muggle would experience.

parselmouth

A Parselmouth is a Wix who can speak and understand Parseltongue, the language of serpents, which makes the Parselmouth able to converse with serpents. This includes serpentine magical creatures, including the Basilisk and the Runespoor. Parseltongue is an inherited trait rather than a learned skill, and is quite rare. The vast majority of Parselmouths are descendants of Salazar Slytherin, though it's possible the trait is not exclusive to Slytherin lineage.

Although Parseltongue is usually spoken with serpents, those who are more proficient in the language can also speak it with each other. Because few who aren't Parselmouths can understand even a little bit of the language, it will just sound like a hissing sound to most people. Albus Dumbledore was the only known exception to being able to recognize and understad Parseltongue without being a Parselmouth, so that is quite rare and should not be possible except for the most advanced Wixen.


nonverbal magic

Non-verbal magic is taught in the sixth and seventh years at Hogwarts in Charms, DADA, and Transfiguration classes. Most Wixen will be proficient in at least some non-verbal magic from then on, because they are required to begin performing magic in these classes non-verbally. It's still worth noting students before their sixth year would be unlikely to know much if any non-verbal magic, and non-verbal magic can still be difficult. It's not recommended to try to cast more advanced spells, such as the Patronus Charm, non-verbally. The success of non-verbal magic can also depend a great deal on a Wix's wand.

If you have questions about this, please don't hesitate to ask. We don't include non-verbal magic among our Abilities claims because it's a relatively common ability, but we wanted members to understand it still has its limits.


wandless magic

Wandless magic is not common in much of the Western world and Asia, though it is more common in Africa, the African diaspora, and among Native Americans. When wands are culturally common, they are used to channel the Wix's magic, and trying to perform magic without them can often have unintended and volatile results. In such cultures, only the most adept Wixen would be able to effectively perform wandless magic. Charms and Transfiguration are particularly difficult without wands.


patronus

The Patronus Charm is one of the most powerful defensive spells in a Wix's arsenal, and one of the most advanced. Being able to cast a fully corporeal Patronus is even more difficult and advanced than casting an incorporeal Patronus. That said, in recent years it has become more common, as it is now being taught as part of the Defense Against the Dark Arts curriculum in a student's sixth and seventh years at Hogwarts. It is now required to be able to cast an incorporeal Patronus for an O on the DADA N.E.W.T., but an E can still be achieved without casting a Patronus.

The Patronus is cast with the incantation Expecto Patronum; it is too advanced a spell to be cast wandlessly or non-verbally except by the most adept Wixen, unless a Wix is from a culture in which wandless magic is common. The Patronus generally relies on the Wix concentrating on their happiest memory while casting the charm. The Patronus can be used to repel Dementors and Lethifolds, and is the only known repellent against them. It can also be used to repel Vampires, along with other known repellents. Finally, it can be used as a form of communication by sending a miniature version of one's Patronus to deliver a verbal message, though this is quite advanced.


legilimency

A Legilimens is a Wix who can magically delve into the thoughts, memories, and emotions of others. Legilimency is usually a learned skill, though it seems some exceptionally talented Wixen have a natural aptitude for it that doesn't require much training in the skill. This would be quite rare.

Legilimency usually requires a wand and use of the incantation Legilimens, though it can be performed non-verbally and wandlessly by the most advanced Legilimens, who may also be capable of planting thoughts, memories, and emotions into the target's mind. Even in the case of advanced Legilimency, the target will likely be aware Legilimency is occurring. Legilimency is easier if the practitioner's and target's eyes meet.

occlumency

An Occlumens is a Wix who can magically close their mind against Legilimency. This can prevent a Legilimens both from seeing the Wix's own thoughts, memories, and emotions, as well as preventing them from planting thoughts, memories, and emotions in the Wix's mind. Occlumency is a learned skill that requires a great deal of willpower, though a Wix may have a natural aptitude for it that makes learning easier.

The most basic method of Occlumency involves clearing the mind of all thoughts, memories, and emotions. A more advanced Occlumens would be able to create a false layer of thoughts, memories, or emotions for the Legilimens to find, so they would be unable to detect the use of Occlumency and believe they've successfully penetrated the Occlumens' mind. This latter method of Occlumency would be possible for only the most advanced Occlumens, not a beginner.

let me in the ring

Times Change

It's not as though fighting was ever unpopular. There have always been dueling clubs in the wixen world. There have always been physical fight enthusiasts, too, keeping boxing gyms and martial arts studios in business and thriving. The urge to battle is simply a part of human nature, and people will always manage to experience it in some way, shape, or form.

It all became very real during the war, though, and afterwards things felt different. For many, there wasn't much of a thrill to be found anymore in the strictly controlled rings they had frequented previously. There were laws, things you simply weren't allowed to do, and plenty of people responded with resistance. They had seen the real thing now, and they were loath to go back to an activity with such low stakes.

Boxers are wix/humans who fight without use of wands (and technically magic). Duelists use their wands in the traditional form of wix dueling. Brawlers are various shapeshifters including werewolves, animagi, and other wix or sentient creature able to take the form of something other than a human in order to fight. Referees act as bouncers and security for both the crowd and the fighters.

Enter the Reviled

And so the anarchists saw an opportunity to profit. They put down money for the development of London's illegal fighting scene and employed established criminals to make it happen. By now, business is booming and the higher-ups are able to rake in quite a fortune.

The arena itself is literally underground, its exact location known only to very few people. Access is granted via a rotating set of several cleverly hidden portkeys and vanishing cabinets. On alternating nights, combatants can participate in magical duels and brutal boxing matches. Gambling is encouraged, and death in the ring is simply accepted as one of the risks.

we've got a bit of a problem

Nox

It started out as a pain potion, and in the aftermath of the war, plenty of people were in need of it. So many were grievously injured, stuck in St. Mungo's for months, writhing in agony as healers worked a mile a minute to develop cures for new curses they had never seen before. Combining an already existing pain potion with the distilled buds of the willoweed bush, a young potioneer created something brand new. She called it pulchrinox, and with it, healers could treat even the most desperate levels of pain.

But the buds from the willoweed bush, as it turned out, are as profoundly addictive as they are powerful. By the time these grievously injured patients were ready to go home from the hospital, they were hooked. A weaning-off treatment was advised, and eventually it was discovered that the other parts of the willoweed bush only contained the painkiller properties and not the addictive ones. Thus pseudonox was developed, an alternative pain potion that, while being a bit less effective, was not habit-forming.

But the damage had already been done. Family members of the injured had quickly begun experimenting with the drug, and they had found that it could ease mental pain as well as physical. Countless numbers of wixen who were suffering from the remnants of their wartime trauma found themselves addicted. And then, eventually, it gained the reputation of being just plain enjoyable.

Lux

By the time private use of the willoweed bush was banned, it had already spread far and wide among those who wanted to profit off of addicts. Less expensive blends of the drug were less pure, meaning more dangerous. The risk of addiction and overdose was absolutely massive. The dealers may not have been using the killing curse, but they were still just as culpable for widespread death and destruction.

And then one of their potioneers had an idea. Their customers were dying because, once they were high on nox, all they wanted to do was take more, to sink further into that euphoria. What if they had another option? What if they created another drug, related to nox but an upper rather than a downer? It was genius. Present them as capable of cancelling each other out, even though in reality mixing them only increased the stress on the user's cardiovascular system. More ways for them to die, but also more ways to profit. To double that profit, even. Thus pulchrilux entered the scene.

Unsurprisingly, by now there is an organized operation functioning beneath the surface of wixen society, devoted to creating and distributing the drug for monetary gain. People at the top are accumulating money at unprecedented rates. To them, it is still the miracle drug. To addicts, it has become their prison and, ultimately, their death.

Stonehenge Academy of Magical Arts

Vires, Artes, Mores

Stonehenge Academy of Magical Arts was founded by Morgan le Fay and Merlin with the motto “Vires, Artes, Mores” (Strength, Skill, Character). It gives young and old wixen alike the opportunity to seek the knowledge of various specialized fields of magic. Stonehenge Academy’s campus is hidden in plain sight a short distance from its namesake in Wiltshire, England. If a muggle looks at the campus, all they see is a collection of run-down barns.

Stonehenge Academy offers a two year program of study for each major. All majors have the option to take an apprenticeship within that specific field for a third year of continued study. There are also options for study beyond a third year and some fields, such as teaching, would require continuous education throughout one's career. Each college has its own building and there are student dormitories located on campus. The dormitories have quad and double layouts, with a limited number of singles available for higher price. The university offers intramural quidditch and has a quidditch pitch for students to have leisurely pick-up games.

Wixen can access the campus through the Floo Network, which arrives in the Administration building; the Knight Bus; or by apparating just outside of the campus grounds.

Fields of study

School of Charms

curses • jinxes • spells • theory

This school focuses on advanced charms work beyond what is learned at secondary schools. Each student will learn not only such advanced charms but the theories underlying them. By the time they complete their two-year course of study, students will have learned the most sophisticated charms, including the patronus charm if they haven't yet mastered it.

School of Defensive Magic

curse breaking • dark magic & creatures • dueling • ward crafting

At this school, students will learn all the advanced skills necessary to become an officer of magical law enforcement, including aurors and hit wix, as well as magic needed for fields such as curse breaking, ward crafting, etc. Beyond the two-year course load, there is the option of a third year apprenticeship in these fields as a substitute for their non-university training programs.

School of Education

classroom management • communication • planning • research and analysis

It's here that students learn the skills they need to become the professors who will teach the next generation, either at secondary schools or universities throughout the world. Most within the School of Education will also take extensive course work within the fields they will be teaching. It is possible to attain simultaneous degrees from the School of Education and one other School at Stonehenge within one additional year depending on the courses taken.

School of Healing

diseases • illnesses • injuries • medications

Students in the School of Healing will be educated in healing magic so they can practice medicine at magical hospitals, schools, or various private practices. There is a mandatory apprenticeship for the School of Healing, usually at St. Mungo's (though the hospital wing of Hogwarts or other private practices may qualify), as a third year program. Healing students often take classes in other fields relevant to healing, including but not limited to herbology and potions.

School of Herbology

botany • equipment • fertilizer • hazards

Herbology students will be prepared for engagement in a variety of fields ranging from magical botany to medicinal herbology and everything in between. The advanced herbology taught at this school will deal with magical plant life that is often rare, exotic, and potentially dangerous. Herbology factors into many other fields so it isn't a surprise to find students of other majors in these classes.

School of Potions

hazards • experimentation • ingredients • instruments

In the School of Potions, students will learn to become more than mere potioneers; the goal for each student will be to prepare them to become potion masters in the years following their advanced education. There are a wide array of uses for potions, including medical uses, use in magical law enforcement, and even tactical use, so students can find themselves going into a wide range of fields after their potions education.

School of Transfiguration

Conjuration • Transformation • Untransfiguration • Vanishment

The main branches of study for transfiguration are the focal point of this school. Each student will learn the intricate theories that eventually brought about each spell. By the time their two-year course of study is complete, each student will have learned some of the subject's most complex transfiguration magic, including the animagus transformation, if they have not mastered it before.

School of Arts and Sciences

Alchemy • Ancient Runes • Arithmancy • Astronomy • Divination • fine arts • History of Magic • Magizoology

This school offers course work in a variety of magical arts and sciences as well as fine arts and muggle studies. Many students whose majors are in other schools will still take mandatory and elective classes from this school relevant to their major fields of study. The school's wandmaking program is a cross-disciplinary program that includes work in herbology and magizoology and has a third year apprenticeship program. The School of Arts and Sciences includes an ever-changing curriculum; the fixed areas of study are listed above.

The New War

McGonagall's Army

What started as a Hogwarts club under the direction of the late Headmistress McGonagall to discuss current affairs within the wixen world, became the moniker of the resistance movement following McGonagall's assassination in 2028. McGonagall's Army seeks to put an end to the Devoted, the Reviled, and Ministry corruption. They are usually outnumbered three-to-one at the very least, but that doesn't stop them from putting their lives on the line to protect the population both magical and muggle in the UK and beyond. Led by the reluctantly-deemed General and commanded by the Brigadiers, they do whatever they can to stop the factions that seek to destroy London. Lieutenants play an active role in McGonagall's Army, and recruiters hold jobs within Hogwarts, Stonehenge, the Ministry, and other places but are always on the lookout for new members and allies, basically serving as spies as well.

Sanctuary

What started as the Society for the Protection and Ethical Treatment of Magical Creatures (S.P.E.T.M.C., bastardized as Spetmac as a mocking reference to Hermione's Spew) under the directive of Hermione Weasley, Ministress for Magic, has now become the Sanctuary, a motley crew of tainted wix, werewolves, and other various sentient creatures who use any means necessary to protect magical creatures. They've taken credit for multiple assassinations of Ministry executioners, and they've made it very clear that they will not stop until all magical creatures are given the freedoms they deserve. The Sanctuary was founded and is led by the militant son of Rubeus Hagrid, Caeruleus Hagrid, who uses brutal tactics along with his dad's old pet hellhound Fluffy as a companion. The Sanctuary consists of three main groups that split into teams of two. The three groups are the werewolves, the wix supporters, and the halfling/tainteds (including but not limited to part-veela and part-giants). The wix are considered hunters/huntresses and usually pair off with a werewolf or a tained as their predator partner. Further, anti-magical creature and tainted legislation has become so strict that the Sanctuary is sometimes forced to take in those who cannot be accepted at schools or the hospital. Therefore, they have their own groups of educators and healers in addition to caretakers the magical creatures taken in for companionship or rehabilitation, thus truly acting like a wildlife sanctuary.

The Scourers

Once a mercenary group that hunted wizards and witches in the 1700s in the early United States, the Scourers have now extended their operations into the UK amid the political turmoil. Led by the Barebone siblings, they offer their services to the highest bidder. Bishops are cold-blooded assassins who tend to take on high-profile targets. Knights are mercenaries, usually operating in units of two or more to provide offensive support. Rooks are some of the most skilled security agents in the world, doing everything from standing bodyguard for high-profile figureheads to guarding major drug deals if necessary. Pawns, contrary to the connotation of the word, are just as important. They are the spies of the Scourers, paid for their ability to stay quiet and stay hidden in order to acquire as much intelligence as possible.
